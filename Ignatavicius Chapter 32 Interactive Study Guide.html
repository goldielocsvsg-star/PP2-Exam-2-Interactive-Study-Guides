Myocardial Infarction (MI) Deep Dive

‚ñº

What is MI?

Injury and necrosis (death) of myocardial tissue due to severe, prolonged oxygen deprivation. Damage starts in the subendocardium (highest O‚ÇÇ demand).

Key Terms & Definitions

‚ñº

Atherosclerosis

Buildup of fatty plaques in artery walls. Primary cause of CAD and ACS.

Ischemia

When blood flow to the heart is blocked, depriving tissue of oxygen. Reversible if treated quickly.

Infarction

Death of heart tissue due to lack of blood flow (oxygen). Irreversible damage.

Troponin

Cardiac enzyme that rises in response to myocardial injury. Key marker for MI diagnosis.

CAD

Coronary Artery Disease - blocked or narrowed coronary arteries due to plaque buildup.

PAOP

Pulmonary Artery Occlusion Pressure - measures pressure in left atrium using catheter to monitor heart function.
