ü´Ä Arterial Terms ‚Äì Tap to Flip!

Atherosclerosis

tap to flip

Plaque buildup inside arteries causing narrowing. Main cause of heart attacks and strokes. Think: "Athero = fatty plaque"

Ankle-Brachial Index (ABI)

tap to flip

Compares BP in ankle vs arm. ABI <0.90 = PAD. Lower number = worse flow. Diabetes may falsely elevate!

Intermittent Claudication

tap to flip

Leg cramping/pain during walking that goes away with rest. Classic sign of PAD! Think: "Claude walks, Claude cramps, Claude rests"

Collateral Circulation

tap to flip

Small new vessels that form to reroute blood around a blockage. Body's "detour route" for blood! Develops over time.

Inflow vs Outflow Disease

tap to flip

Inflow: Above groin (aorta/iliac) ‚Üí pain in back, buttocks, thighs
Outflow: Below groin ‚Üí pain in calves, feet, toes

Arterial Ulcers

tap to flip

Painful sores from poor arterial flow. Found on toes/top of foot. Deep, round, pale, well-defined edges. Poor healing!

Thrombus vs Embolus

Heparin-Induced Thrombocytopenia (HIT)

Dangerous immune reaction to heparin. Paradox: platelets drop but clot risk INCREASES! Stop heparin immediately!

IVC Filter

tap to flip

Filter placed in inferior vena cava to trap clots before reaching lungs. Used when anticoagulants can't be given.

Venous Insufficiency

tap to flip

Veins can't return blood properly ‚Üí pooling ‚Üí swelling, skin changes, stasis ulcers. Caused by faulty valves.

üîÑ How the Body Regulates BP

üéØ Baroreceptors ‚ñº

Sense pressure changes in blood vessels and adjust heart rate & vessel tone. Like a thermostat for BP!

üíß Kidneys ‚ñº

Control fluid and sodium levels. More fluid = higher BP. That's why diuretics work!

üîß RAAS System ‚ñº

Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System increases BP by tightening vessels and retaining sodium. ACE inhibitors and ARBs block this!

üí°
In chronic HTN, these regulatory systems stop working properly over time.

  • Blurred vision

‚ö° Critical Point!

üíä Management

  • Regular exercise
  • Weight control
  • Smoking cessation
  • Stress management
  • Limit alcohol
