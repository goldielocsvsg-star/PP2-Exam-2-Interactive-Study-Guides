üî¨ Lipoprotein Classes

Cholesterol & triglycerides aren't water soluble - lipoproteins allow transport in blood!

VLDL

Very-Low-Density Lipoprotein

  • Major lipid: Triglycerides
  • Source: Liver
  • Function: Delivers TGs to muscle & adipose
  • Apolipoprotein: B-100
‚ö†Ô∏è TGs >500 mg/dL ‚Üí pancreatitis risk!

LDL ‚ö†Ô∏è MOST DANGEROUS

Low-Density Lipoprotein

  • Major lipid: Cholesterol
  • Contains 60-70% of total blood cholesterol
  • Function: Delivers cholesterol to tissues
  • Apolipoprotein: B-100
üìå 1% ‚Üì LDL ‚Üí ~1% ‚Üì CV events!

HDL ‚úÖ PROTECTIVE

High-Density Lipoprotein

  • Major lipid: Cholesterol
  • Function: Reverse cholesterol transport
  • Returns cholesterol TO liver
  • Apolipoproteins: A-I, A-II
‚úÖ Higher HDL = Lower ASCVD risk

üí° Apolipoprotein Memory Trick

B-100 = "Bad" delivery ‚Üí Delivers lipids TO tissues (VLDL, LDL)
A-I = "Away" ‚Üí Takes cholesterol AWAY to liver (HDL)
